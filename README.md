# FAST-Track

A youth-led, community health education platform that teaches stroke symptom
recognition (the FAST protocol — Face, Arm, Speech, Time) to underserved
communities, and trains young people to become local health ambassadors who
carry that knowledge back into their own neighborhoods.

## Status
🚧 In development — capstone project for the FlyRank AI Internship (Frontend
Engineering track), weeks 1–8.

## The problem

In many low-resource settings, stroke outcomes are worsened not by a lack of
willingness to seek help, but by a lack of *recognition* — symptoms go
unnoticed or misread until it's too late to act within the window that
matters most. This gap is widest exactly where diagnostic capacity (CT
scanners, radiologists, stroke units) is scarcest. Public symptom literacy
is one of the few interventions that costs almost nothing and saves lives
regardless of what diagnostic infrastructure is or isn't available nearby.

## What this project does

- **Symptom education** — a clear, accessible walkthrough of the FAST
  protocol, localized into Ghanaian languages (starting with Twi), designed
  to be understood at a glance and shared easily
- **Community Health Ambassador track** — short learning modules plus a
  quiz, ending in a shareable certificate, aimed at young people who want to
  run awareness sessions in their own communities
- **Data storytelling** — a simple, honest visualization of stroke burden
  and diagnostic-access disparities using open public health data, to
  explain *why* this matters before asking anyone to act on it

## Project goals

- [ ] Ship a working, accessible, localized symptom-education flow
- [ ] Ship the Ambassador learning track with a completion certificate
- [ ] Ship a data-storytelling section grounded in real public data
- [ ] Deploy publicly and keep it free and open to underserved communities

## Guiding principles

This project is built as a **nonprofit, save-lives-first initiative** — the
priority is reach and clarity, not monetization. Any future funding or
income-generation path (e.g. sponsorships, grants, or a light
"support this platform" option) will be considered only as a way to sustain
and expand free access for the communities it serves, never as a
prerequisite for using it.
